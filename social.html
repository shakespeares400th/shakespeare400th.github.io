Shakespeare's Influence on our social lives and beliefs

William Shakespeare also affected the many social lives of citizens now and at earlier times.

Shakespeare also the public to understand many social issues. For example, in Romeo and Juliet, Shakespeare was able to teach the public more about human and teenager emotions, such as angst (with Rosaline). Moreover, Shakespeare raised question about prejudice and race. For example, his play 'Othello' had many themes of racism, love, jealously, betrayal and revenge and suggested many questions about these concepts. This play also has been run for 296 shows on Broadway, the longest ever for a Shakespeare play. These plays helped to encourage and widen an audience's acceptance for equality, especially in Shakespeare's time when equality was rare between different people.

Moreover, William Shakespeare also helped to education those about history and bring excitement to these topics, such as the Romans ('Julius Caesar'), English monarchs, prominent women: Juliet, Ophelia, Kate the shrew, Cleopatra and Richard II, III and Henry V. These helped to make the public aware of key people and to bring the concept to a personal level for increased understanding and also to inspire others.

In addition, Shakespeare is thought to have inspired many names. These include Simpson, Biel, Rabbit and Jessica, which originally appeared in the play 'The Merchant of Venice'.
